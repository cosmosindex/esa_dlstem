## Ground Sampling Distance (GSD) Notes

The SatSOT paper does not explicitly state the image GSD (ground sampling distance, in m/pixel). However, several indirect cues can be used to estimate it:

- **Original satellite video frames are 12,000 x 5,000 pixels**; each sequence is a crop from these full frames.
- **Satellite sources:** Jilin-1, Skybox (Planet), and Carbonite-2.
- **Frame rate:** 10 or 25 fps; each video clip is approximately 30 seconds long.
- **Target sizes are very small:** typical objects (cars, ships) are under 10 pixels across. The mean bounding box area for cars is only 112.4 px² (roughly 10 x 11 pixels), indicating coarse spatial resolution.

Based on publicly available specifications of these satellite platforms:

| Satellite | Operator | Video-Mode GSD (approx.) |
|---|---|---|
| Jilin-1 | Chang Guang Satellite Technology | ~0.92–1.1 m/pixel |
| SkySat (Skybox) | Planet Labs | ~0.9–1.0 m/pixel |
| Carbonite-2 | Surrey Satellite Technology (SSTL) | ~1.0 m/pixel |

**Estimated GSD for SatSOT: approximately 0.9–1.1 m/pixel** (video mode). This is consistent with the observed small target sizes — a typical car (~4 m x 2 m) would occupy roughly 4 x 2 = 8 pixels at 1 m/pixel GSD, which aligns with the reported bounding box statistics.

For exact GSD values, consult the respective satellite platform technical documentation or contact the dataset authors.
